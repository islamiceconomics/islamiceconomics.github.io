## LinkedIn

Fragmented payment systems are imposing tangible costs across the Muslim world. When entrepreneurs and charities move funds across OIC borders, intermediaries and conversions can consume up to 7% of the value. With remittances to OIC countries exceeding $400 billion in 2023 and intra-OIC trade around $600 billion, the aggregate drain is substantial: the average intra-OIC remittance cost is 6.8%, nearly double the UN SDG target of 3%, representing an annual loss of over $27 billion. This article argues for a unified OIC digital payments infrastructure that could lower transaction costs, embed Shariah-compliant commercial principles (such as transparency and fairness), and reallocate resources toward development, poverty alleviation, and entrepreneurship. The piece situates the case within both economic data and Islamic norms that encourage cooperation and fair commerce.
