Historical Islamic Economics

A narrative journey through 1,500 years of Islamic economic thought — from the markets of pre-Islamic Mecca through the golden age of scholars and traders to the colonial disruption and modern revival. Engaging, story-driven, and historically rich.

🎧 6 episodes ⏱ ~17 min each Hosts: Tariq & Amira
01
The Forgotten Economists
A 1,500-year journey through Islamic economic thought — from Mecca to Ibn Khaldun to the modern revival.
14 min ▶ Play

Did you know that a 14th-century Muslim scholar described supply and demand, the division of labor, and the labor theory of value — four hundred years before Adam Smith? In this pilot episode, we trace the entire arc of Islamic economic thought: from the bustling markets of pre-Islamic Mecca and the Prophet Muhammad's career as a merchant, through the golden age of scholars like Abu Yusuf, Al-Ghazali, and Ibn Khaldun, to the colonial disruption and the remarkable modern revival of Islamic finance.

Pre-Islamic Trade Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) Riba & Zakat Golden Age Scholars Ibn Khaldun Trade Networks Modern Revival
02
The Marketplace of Medina
How the Prophet established the first regulated Islamic marketplace — fair trade, consumer protection, and the hisbah system.
17 min ▶ Play

When Prophet Muhammad arrived in Medina in 622 CE, the marketplace was a place where the clever merchant won and ordinary people lost. He transformed it entirely — establishing open access, banning sham bidding and hoarding, and creating the hisbah system of market inspectors. Discover how 7th-century marketplace reforms created the foundation for Islamic commercial law.

Medina Marketplace Hisbah System Najash Fair Weights Consumer Protection Khiyar
03
The House of Wisdom
Baghdad's golden age — cheques, bills of exchange, venture capital, and banking without interest.
15 min ▶ Play

In the 9th century, Baghdad had cheques, bills of exchange, international money transfers, and venture capital — all without interest. Explore how Caliph Harun al-Rashid built the world's first multicultural economic hub, how Abu Yusuf revolutionized tax policy, and how the hawala system moved money across continents on trust alone.

Abbasid Baghdad Harun al-Rashid Abu Yusuf Sakk & Suftaja Hawala Mudaraba
04
Coins of the Caliphate
The gold dinar, silver dirham, and the monetary revolution that unified Islamic commerce across continents.
20 min ▶ Play

In 696 CE, Caliph Abd al-Malik ibn Marwan made a bold decision: replace the Byzantine and Persian coins circulating in Islamic lands with purely Islamic coinage. The gold dinar and silver dirham became the most trusted currencies of the medieval world. Discover how Al-Ghazali articulated the principle later known as Gresham's Law, and how Al-Maqrizi conducted one of history's first studies of inflation.

Gold Dinar Silver Dirham Abd al-Malik Al-Ghazali Ibn Taymiyyah Inflation
05
Caravans to Cathay
The vast Islamic trade networks — from Andalusia to China — held together by trust, law, and shared faith.
16 min ▶ Play

In 1324, Mansa Musa of Mali crossed the Sahara with 60,000 people and so much gold that he crashed the precious metals market in Cairo. His journey was just one moment in a much larger story: the greatest pre-modern trading bloc in history. Journey across the Silk Road, the Indian Ocean dhow routes, and the trans-Saharan gold trade with Ibn Battuta as our guide.

Silk Road Indian Ocean Trade Mansa Musa Ibn Battuta Trans-Saharan Trade Amana (Trust)
06
The Waqf — A Thousand Years of Charity
How the Islamic endowment system funded hospitals, universities, and cities for centuries — and what happened when colonialism dismantled it.
21 min ▶ Play

By the Ottoman peak, an estimated one-third of all productive land was waqf property — irrevocable charitable endowments funding free hospitals, universities, soup kitchens, and water infrastructure. The Süleymaniye Complex, Al-Azhar University, and countless others operated for centuries on this principle. Then European colonial powers systematically dismantled it. This is the story of institutional memory, loss, and potential recovery.

Waqf Endowments Al-Azhar University Süleymaniye Complex Ottoman Welfare Colonial Dismantlement Modern Revival
