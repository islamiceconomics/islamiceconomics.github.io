Appendix B — Quranic & Hadith References

Scriptural Foundations of Islamic Economics

Quranic & Hadith References

This appendix organizes key Quranic verses and hadith traditions by topic, providing scriptural foundations for Islamic economic principles discussed throughout the book.

B.1 Riba (Interest/Usury)

Quranic Verses:

  • Quran 2:275: “Those who consume riba will not stand except as one whom Satan has knocked down… Allah has permitted commerce and forbidden riba.”

  • Quran 3:130: “O you who believe! Do not consume riba, multiplied many times over. Fear Allah that you might be successful.”

  • Quran 4:161: “And for their consuming riba when they were forbidden from it, and their consuming the wealth of people unjustly.”

  • Quran 30:39: “That which you give as riba so it may increase the wealth of people will not increase it with Allah. But that which you give as zakat, desiring the countenance of Allah—those are the ones who will multiply.”

Hadith:

  • Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: “Every loan that brings a benefit is a form of riba.” (Sunan Ibn Majah)

B.2 Zakat (Obligatory Alms)

Quranic Verses:

  • Quran 2:43: “Establish prayer and give zakat, and bow with those who bow.”

  • Quran 2:110: “And establish prayer and give zakat. Whatever good you put forward for yourselves, you will find it with Allah.”

  • Quran 9:60: “Zakat is only for the poor, the needy, those employed to collect it, those whose hearts are to be reconciled, those in bondage, those in debt, in the cause of Allah, and the traveler—an obligation from Allah.”

  • Quran 51:19: “In their wealth is a share for those who ask and those who are deprived.”

Hadith:

  • Prophet Muhammad said: “Zakat is one of the five pillars of Islam.” (Sahih Bukhari)

B.3 Trade and Commerce

Quranic Verses:

  • Quran 2:275: “Allah has permitted commerce and forbidden riba.”

  • Quran 62:10: “When the prayer has been concluded, disperse within the land and seek from the bounty of Allah, and remember Allah often that you might succeed.”

  • Quran 16:90: “Indeed, Allah orders justice and good conduct and giving to relatives, and forbids immorality and bad conduct and oppression.”

  • Quran 4:29: “O you who believe! Do not consume one another’s wealth unjustly but only through business by mutual consent.”

Hadith:

  • Prophet Muhammad said: “The best earnings are those of a trader who deals justly in buying and selling.” (Al-Tirmidhi)

B.4 Justice and Fairness

Quranic Verses:

  • Quran 5:8: “O you who believe! Be just witnesses for Allah, even if it be against yourselves or your parents or relatives.”

  • Quran 16:76: “And do not approach the property of the orphan except in the way that is best, until he reaches maturity.”

  • Quran 83:1-3: “Woe to those who use short measure—those who, when they take a measure from people, take in full. But if they give by measure or by weight to others, they cause loss.”

  • Quran 6:152: “And give full measure when you measure, and weigh with a balance that is straight.”

B.5 Poverty and Compassion

Quranic Verses:

  • Quran 90:12-16: “What will make you know what is al-’aqabah? It is the freeing of a slave, and the feeding in times of hunger of an orphaned relative or a poor person in misery.”

  • Quran 93:10: “As for the orphan, do not oppress him. And as for the asker, do not repel him.”

  • Quran 76:8-9: “They give food in spite of love for it to the poor, the orphan, and the captive: We feed you only for the countenance of Allah.”

  • Quran 107:1-7: “Have you seen the one who denies the Day of Judgment? For that is the one who drives away the orphan and does not encourage the feeding of the poor.”

B.6 Spending and Moderation

Quranic Verses:

  • Quran 17:26-27: “And give to relatives their due, and to the poor and to the traveler, and do not spend wastefully. Indeed, the wasteful are brothers of the devils, and ever has Satan been to his Lord ungrateful.”

  • Quran 25:67: “And those who, when they spend, are not extravagant and not stingy, but hold a just balance between those extremes.”

  • Quran 2:219: “They ask you about wine and gambling. Say: In them is great sin and some benefit for people, but their sin is greater than their benefit.”

B.7 Wealth and Possessions

Quranic Verses:

  • Quran 3:14: “Beautified for people is the love of that which they desire—of women and sons, heaped-up sums of gold and silver, fine branded horses, and cattle and tilled land. That is the enjoyment of worldly life, but Allah has with Him the best return.”

  • Quran 63:9: “O you who believe! Let not your wealth and your children divert you from the remembrance of Allah. And whoever does that—then those are the losers.”

  • Quran 16:55-57: “He distributes His mercy to whom He wills among His servants… Yet when they taste His mercy, they become delighted with it.”

B.8 Work and Earning

Quranic Verses:

  • Quran 53:39: “And that there is not for a man except that for which he strives.”

  • Quran 9:105: “And say: Work; Allah will see your work, and His Messenger and the believers.”

Hadith:

  • Prophet Muhammad said: “No one eats better food than that which he eats out of his work, and verily the Prophet of Allah, David, used to eat from the earnings of his manual labor.” (Sahih Bukhari)

B.9 Contracts and Business

Quranic Verses:

  • Quran 5:1: “O you who believe! Fulfill [all] contracts.”

  • Quran 2:282-283: “O you who believe! When you contract a debt for a specified term, write it down… And if you are on a journey and cannot find a scribe, then let there be a pledge taken.”

Hadith:

  • Prophet Muhammad said: “The best among you are those who are best to their families, and I am the best among you to my family. Whoever purchases a slave and teaches him good manners and disciplines him properly and then frees him, will have an enormous reward.” (At-Tirmidhi)
