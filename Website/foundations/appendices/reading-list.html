Appendix C — Recommended Reading

Essential Works in Islamic Economics

Recommended Reading

This reading list provides curated selections of foundational, contemporary, and specialized works in Islamic economics, organized by topic for easy reference.

C.1 Foundational Works in Islamic Economics

  1. Chapra, Muhammad Umer. The Islamic Welfare State and Its Role in the Economy. Leicester: Islamic Foundation, 1992.
    • Comprehensive exploration of the Islamic state’s role in economic management and social welfare.
  2. Khan, Muhammad Akram. Islamic Economics and Finance: A Glossary. London: Routledge, 2nd edition, 2003.
    • Accessible reference work defining key concepts and terminology in Islamic economics.
  3. Kahf, Monzer. Islamic Economics: Notes on Definition and Methodology. Journal of Research in Islamic Economics, 1989.
    • Methodological foundation for understanding Islamic economics as a distinct discipline.
  4. Maududi, Abu A’la. Economic System of Islam. Lahore: Islamic Publications, 1984.
    • Classic exposition of Islamic economic principles from a prominent Islamic scholar.
  5. Naqvi, Syed Nawab Haider. Ethics and Economics: An Islamic Synthesis. Leicester: Islamic Foundation, 1981.
    • Integration of ethical principles with economic analysis.

C.2 Microeconomics and Consumer Theory

  1. Ahmad, Khurshid (ed.). Contemporary Practices of Islamic Financing Techniques. Jeddah: IRTI-IDB, 2002.
    • Overview of modern Islamic financial instruments and their applications.
  2. El-Gamal, Mahmoud A. Islamic Finance: Law, Economics, and Practice. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2006.
    • Rigorous economic and legal analysis of Islamic finance mechanisms.
  3. Iqbal, Zamir & Mirakhor, Abbas. An Introduction to Islamic Finance: Theory and Practice. Singapore: Wiley, 2011.
    • Comprehensive textbook on Islamic financial instruments and markets.
  4. Siddiqui, Muhammad Nejatullah. Islamic Economic Thought. Lahore: Islamic Book Foundation, 1988.
    • Systematic presentation of Islamic economic theory and concepts.

C.3 Money, Banking, and Finance

  1. Ariff, Muhammad (ed.). Islamic Banking and Finance: New Perspectives on Profit-Sharing and Risk. Cheltenham: Edward Elgar, 2011.
    • Advanced analysis of profit-sharing mechanisms and risk management in Islamic finance.
  2. Iqbal, Zamir. Zakah, Moderation and Aggregate Demand. IMF Staff Papers, Vol. 44, No. 4, 1997.
    • Macroeconomic analysis of zakat’s role in stabilization.
  3. Jouaber-Snoussi, Karim & Abdelsalam, Omneya (eds.). Islamic Banking and Finance: New Challenges and Opportunities. London: Routledge, 2015.
    • Contemporary issues and future developments in Islamic banking.
  4. Mirakhor, Abbas. Towards an Islamic Macroeconomic Model for Muslim Economies. The American Journal of Islamic Social Sciences, Vol. 8, 1991.
    • Framework for macroeconomic analysis in Islamic context.

C.4 Waqf and Social Economics

  1. Benthall, Jonathan & Bellion-Jourdan, Jérôme. The Charitable Crescent: A History of the World’s Muslimlands and Their Voluntary Sector. London: I.B. Tauris, 2003.
    • Historical perspective on Islamic charitable institutions and civil society.
  2. Cizakca, Murat. A History of Philanthropic Foundations: The Islamic World from the Seventh Century to the Present. Istanbul: Boğaziçi University Press, 2000.
    • Comprehensive history of waqf and endowment institutions in Islamic civilization.
  3. Kahf, Monzer. The Role of Waqf in Improving the Ummah. Paper presented to the International Seminar on Waqf as an Instrument of Social Development, 2003.
    • Analysis of waqf’s potential in contemporary Muslim societies.
  4. Kuran, Timur. The Long Divergence: How Islamic Law Held Back the Middle East. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2011.
    • Critical perspective on institutional development and economic stagnation.

C.5 Development and Sustainability

  1. Asutay, Mehmet. Conceptualising and Operationalising Social Maslaha for Islamic Finance. ISRA International Journal of Islamic Finance, Vol. 4, No. 1, 2012.
    • Social welfare in Islamic finance framework.
  2. Khan, Muhammad Akram & Mirakhor, Abbas (eds.). Islamic Economics and Finance. New York: Routledge, 2016.
    • Contemporary perspectives on Islamic economics and development.
  3. Siddiqi, Muhammad Nejatullah. Muslim Economic Thinking: A Survey of Contemporary Literature. Leicester: Islamic Foundation, 1981.
    • Survey of contemporary Islamic economic thought and debates.
  4. UNDP. Islamic Finance and Human Development Report. New York: United Nations Development Programme, various years.
    • Analysis of Islamic finance’s role in sustainable development and human progress.

C.6 Critiques and Comparative Analysis

  1. Askari, Hossein; Iqbal, Zamir; & Mirakhor, Abbas. New Issues in Islamic Finance and Economics: Progress and Challenges. Singapore: Wiley, 2015.
    • Critical examination of contemporary challenges and debates in Islamic finance.
  2. Chapra, Muhammad Umer. Islam and the Economic Challenge. Leicester: Islamic Foundation, 1992.
    • Analysis of economic challenges facing Islamic societies and potential solutions.
  3. Henry, Clement M. & Wilson, Rodney (eds.). The Politics of Islamic Finance. Edinburgh: Edinburgh University Press, 2004.
    • Political economy perspectives on Islamic finance development and regulation.

C.7 Key Journal Articles and Working Papers

  1. Islamic Economics, Banking & Finance (academic journal collection)
    • Regular publications in: Journal of Islamic Economics, Banking and Finance, Islamic Finance Review, ISRA International Journal of Islamic Finance, Nuance, Review of Islamic Economics.
  2. Islamic Research and Training Institute (IRTI). Working Papers and Policy Briefs Series.
    • Accessible research from Islamic Development Bank’s research arm covering current topics.
  3. Centre for Islamic Economics and Finance (CIEF). Research Papers and Discussion Notes.
    • University of Cambridge and other institutions’ publications on Islamic economics.

C.8 Digital Resources

  • Islamic Finance.com: Comprehensive news, research, and industry information
  • Thomson Reuters Islamic Finance Directory: Global database of Islamic finance institutions and products
  • AAOIFI Standards: International Shari’ah standards for Islamic financial institutions
  • IDB Group: Publications and research from the Islamic Development Bank
