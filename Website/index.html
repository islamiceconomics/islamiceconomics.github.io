What We Cover

Eight pillars of comprehensive Islamic economic knowledge

Foundations

Theological and conceptual foundations from Quran, Sunnah, and Fiqh, covering riba, zakat, waqf, and the spiritual underpinnings of Islamic economics.

Scriptural Sources

Searchable database of Quranic ayahs and authentic ahadith on economic topics, cross-referenced by theme and interconnected.

Formal Theory

Rigorous mathematical foundations, from preference axioms to general equilibrium, translating Islamic principles into formal economic theory.

Historical Economics

Economic history of Islamic civilizations: monetary systems, trade networks, inflation, and institutional development across centuries.

Academic Research

Original quantitative research including DSGE models, impulse response analysis, and policy simulations for interest-free economies.

Economic Dashboard

Monitoring, outlook, and DSGE analysis for major OIC economies, including GDP, inflation, debt, trade, and structural models.

Media

Podcasts, videos, and documentaries exploring Islamic economic history, principles, and their modern-day relevance.
