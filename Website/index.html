What We Cover

Eight pillars of comprehensive Islamic economic knowledge

Foundations

Theological and conceptual foundations from Quran, Sunnah, and Fiqh — riba, zakat, waqf, and the spiritual underpinnings of Islamic economics.

Scriptural Sources

Searchable database of Quranic ayahs and authentic ahadith on economic topics — cross-referenced by theme and interconnected.

Formal Theory

Rigorous mathematical foundations — from preference axioms to general equilibrium — translating Islamic principles into formal economic theory.

Historical Economics

Economic history of Islamic civilizations — monetary systems, trade networks, inflation, and institutional development across centuries.

Academic Research
