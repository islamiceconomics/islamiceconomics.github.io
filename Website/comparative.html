Comparative Religious Economics

How the world's great faiths approach economic life: usury, charity, wealth, labour, and justice across traditions

Usury and Interest

From absolute prohibition to gradual acceptance: how Islam, Christianity, and Judaism have navigated the ethics of lending at interest across centuries.

Charity and Obligation

Zakat, tithing, tzedakah: the spectrum from voluntary giving to mandatory redistribution, and what each tradition demands of the wealthy.

Wealth and Poverty

The camel through the eye of the needle versus 'the upper hand is better than the lower hand': fundamentally different theologies of prosperity.

Just Price and Fair Trade

Aquinas and the Scholastics, Islamic market ethics, and Talmudic commercial law: three traditions wrestling with what makes a price just.

Labour and Wages

The dignity of work, the right to fair compensation, and the obligation of employers: comparative teachings on economic justice in the workplace.

Property and Ownership

Stewardship versus dominion: how different faiths understand the relationship between people, possessions, and the divine.

Content is being developed. Each theme will feature detailed analysis with primary source references from the Quran, Bible, Torah, and scholarly traditions.
